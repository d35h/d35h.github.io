Welcome to my home in Holešovice!

Wondering where to eat, drink, and explore nearby? Here’s my hand‑picked list:

Coffee & Breakfast

  • Osada: Specialty coffee, farm‑to‑table brunch & a spacious courtyard garden. Osadní 35 — Mon‑Fri 8‑20, Sat–Sun 9‑16.

Czech Classics

  • Pod Párou: Modern Czech pub fare in a converted 1909 steam‑mill. Jankovcova 49 — kitchen till midnight, great Pilsner on tap.

Barbecue Fix

  • Big Smokers: Texas‑style BBQ: brisket, ribs, pulled pork smoked on site. Dělnická 40 — Wed‑Sat 11‑22, Sun 11‑18.

International Eats

  • Mexická: Authentic Mexican & Latin‑American plates—ceviche, tacos, mezcal cocktails. U Průhonu 13 — Mon–Wed 11‑23, Thu–Fri 11‑24, Sat 11:30‑23, Sun 11:30‑22.

Hang‑Out Spots

  • Vnitroblock: Hip industrial hub with café, cocktail bar, design shops & events. Enter from Tusarova or Dělnická streets.

Farmers Market

  • Pražská Tržnice: Huge riverside market for fresh produce, street food & vintage finds. Bubenské nábř. 13 — Weekdays 7:30‑17, Sat till 14:00.

Landmark Strolls

  • HolKa Footbridge: Elegant 300 m pedestrian & cycle bridge to Karlín; bronze railings glow after dark.
  • DOX Centre for Contemporary Art: Cutting‑edge exhibitions plus the wooden ‘Gulliver’ airship rooftop. Poupětova 1 — Tue–Sun 11‑19.

Parks & Views

  • Letná Park: Hill‑top beer garden & giant Metronome with sunset panoramas over Prague’s bridges.
  • Stromovka: Prague’s “Central Park”: 100 ha of ponds, lawns & leafy avenues for jogging, picnics and lazy afternoons.
